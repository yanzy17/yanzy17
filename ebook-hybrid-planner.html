Kenapa Hybrid Worker Butuh Sistem?

Lo pernah nggak ngerasa hari ini produktif banget di kantor, tapi besoknya WFH malah bubar semua? Atau sebaliknya — WFH flow banget, tapi begitu masuk office malah kena meeting marathon?

🎯 Masalah Utama Hybrid Worker:

  • Context Switching Overload — Otak lo harus reset setiap kali pindah environment (rumah → kantor → cafe)
  • No Consistent Structure — Rutinitas yang works di rumah nggak works di kantor, dan sebaliknya
  • Meeting Fragmentation — Di kantor kena meeting fisik, WFH kena Zoom marathon
  • Boundary Blur — Kapan mulai kerja? Kapan selesai? Siapa yang tau?
  • Tool Scattered — Notes di sini, task di sana, calendar beda lagi
Hybrid work bukan soal "kerja di mana", tapi soal "sistem apa yang bisa adapt di mana pun lo berada."

04 bab 1

Mindset Hybrid Worker

The Unique Challenges & Mental Models

Hybrid work bukan "WFH + Office Day." Ini adalah skill baru yang perlu dipelajari — sama seperti lo belajar tools baru.

🧠 3 Tipe Hari dalam Hybrid Work

🏢 Office Days

Collaboration-Heavy

Optimized for: Meetings, brainstorming, 1-on-1s, team bonding, spontaneous conversations

Mindset shift: "Hari ini gue invest di relationships dan decisions yang butuh face-time."

Pitfall: Jangan coba deep work di kantor yang open-plan. Embrace the chaos.

🏠 WFH Days

Deep Work Focus

Optimized for: Deep work, creative tasks, writing, coding, strategic thinking

Mindset shift: "Hari ini gue protect focus time. Setiap interruption = debt."

Pitfall: Jangan isi WFH day dengan back-to-back Zoom calls. Itu bukan WFH, itu office di rumah.

🚀 Flex/Travel Days

Administrative & Planning

Optimized for: Admin tasks, planning, light meetings, learning, documentation

Mindset shift: "Hari ini gue clear the deck. Process inbox, plan next week, update docs."

Pitfall: Jangan expect high-output day. Flex days itu maintenance mode — dan itu OK.

⚡ Energy Management > Time Management

Produktivitas bukan soal berapa JAM lo kerja. Tapi soal di JAM BERAPA lo melakukan JENIS PEKERJAAN yang tepat.

Energy LevelJenis KerjaContoh
🔋 Peak (100%)Creative & StrategicWriting, coding complex features, decision-making
🔋 High (75%)CollaborativeMeetings, brainstorming, code review
🔋 Medium (50%)StructuredEmail, documentation, data entry
🔋 Low (25%)AdministrativeFiling, scheduling, light reading

🔄 The Hybrid Worker Mental Model

  1. Plan by Day Type — Setiap minggu, assign "type" ke setiap hari. Jangan campur aduk intentionnya.
  2. Match Energy to Task — Peak hours untuk deep work. Low energy untuk admin. Always.
  3. Build Transition Rituals — Ritual pindah mode (rumah → kantor, kerja → istirahat) mencegah bleed-over.
  4. Embrace Asymmetry — Nggak semua hari harus sama productive. Some days are investment days.
  5. Single System, Multiple Contexts — Satu tools/system yang works di semua environment. Notion is perfect for this.

🎯 Actionable Takeaway Bab 1

Minggu depan, coba label setiap hari kerja lo dengan: 🏢 Office / 🏠 WFH / 🚀 Flex. Lalu tentukan TOP 1 intention per hari berdasarkan tipe itu. Track selama 1 minggu dan lihat perbedaannya.

Hour-by-Hour Templates untuk Setiap Tipe Hari

A day without a plan is a day donated to other people's priorities.

🏠 Template: WFH Day

WFH day = deep work paradise. Protect it.

06:00 - 07:00

🌅 Morning Routine

Wake up, no phone 30 min, movement (stretch/walk/exercise), shower, healthy breakfast

07:00 - 07:15

📋 Daily Planning

Open Notion → Daily Log → Set MIT 1-2-3, check calendar, set energy level, choose day type

07:15 - 09:30

🧠 Deep Work Block #1

MIT 1 — Pekerjaan paling penting. Phone silent. Notifications off. Focus mode ON. No email, no Slack.

Technique: 2x 60-min focus sessions + 15-min break di tengah

09:30 - 10:00

☕ Buffer Block

Coffee break. Quick Slack check. Reply urgent only. Stretch. Refill water.

10:00 - 12:00

🧠 Deep Work Block #2

MIT 2 — Second priority task. Same rules: focus mode, no notifications. Use Pomodoro if energy dipping (25/5 cycles).

12:00 - 13:00

🍽️ Lunch Break

ACTUAL break. Leave desk. Eat mindfully. Walk 10 min. No screen if possible.

13:00 - 14:30

💬 Meeting Window

Kalau ada meeting, schedule di sini. Kalau nggak ada, ini jadi MIT 3 time.

14:30 - 16:00

📧 Shallow Work Block

Email batch processing, Slack replies, documentation, admin tasks, code review

16:00 - 16:30

🔄 Shutdown Routine

Update Notion Daily Log, set tomorrow's priorities, close all tabs, write "Win of the Day"

Weekly Planning System

Sunday Planning Ritual (45 Minutes)

Give me six hours to chop down a tree and I'll spend the first four sharpening the axe. — Planning is sharpening.

📅 The Sunday Planning Ritual

Setiap Minggu malam (atau Senin pagi kalau lo prefer), invest 45 menit untuk plan the week. Ini yang bikin difference antara reactive vs intentional.

⏱️ 45-Minute Planning Breakdown

  1. Review Last Week (10 min) — Buka Weekly Review template. Score minggu lalu 1-5. Tulis 3 wins dan 1 lesson. Jangan skip ini — reflection = compound growth.
  2. Brain Dump (5 min) — Tulis SEMUA yang ada di kepala lo. Tasks, worries, ideas, follow-ups. Jangan filter. Just dump everything out.
  3. Calendar Scan (5 min) — Review semua meetings & commitments minggu depan. Identify: mana yang bisa di-cancel/delegate? Mana yang butuh prep?
  4. Assign Day Types (5 min) — Label setiap hari: Office / WFH / Flex. Based on meetings, deadlines, dan energy needs.
  5. Set Weekly MIT (10 min) — Pilih 3 outcomes yang HARUS selesai minggu ini. Bukan tasks — outcomes. "Finish proposal draft" bukan "work on proposal."
  6. Time Block (10 min) — Block deep work, meetings, buffer time di calendar. Protect your focus blocks seperti protect meetings penting.

Time Blocking Mastery

4 Types of Time Blocks & How to Protect Them

What gets scheduled gets done. What doesn't get blocked gets stolen by other people's priorities.

🧱 4 Types of Time Blocks

🧠 1. Deep Work Block

90-120 min

Purpose: Complex, creative, strategic tasks yang butuh full concentration.

Rules:

  • No notifications — airplane mode
  • Single task only — no multitasking
  • Pre-decide what you'll work on
  • Tell team you're unavailable

Best time: First 2-3 hours after waking (peak energy)

📧 2. Shallow Work Block

45-60 min

Purpose: Admin, emails, Slack, quick tasks, routine work yang nggak butuh deep thinking.

Rules:

  • Batch similar tasks together
  • Set timer — don't let it expand
  • Use checklists for efficiency
  • Process, don't just check

Best time: Post-lunch atau late afternoon (lower energy)

⏸️ 3. Buffer Block

15-30 min

Purpose: Transition between activities, unexpected tasks, overflow from other blocks.

Rules:

  • Place between every 2 blocks
  • Don't pre-assign tasks to it
  • Use for: stretch, water, bathroom, quick replies
  • If not needed, bonus rest!

Best time: Between every major block transition

🌿 4. Recovery Block

30-60 min

Purpose: Recharge energy. Walk, nap, meditation, non-work activity.

Rules:

  • Non-negotiable — treat like a meeting
  • No screens if possible
  • Active recovery > passive scrolling
  • At least 1 per day

Best time: After lunch or mid-afternoon energy dip

🛡️ How to Protect Time Blocks

  1. Block in Calendar as "Busy" — Semua time block masuk calendar. Deep work = "Focus Time (Do Not Disturb)." Orang lihat lo busy, they won't book.
  2. Communicate to Team — "Hey team, 08-10 setiap hari gue focus time. Reach me after 10." Simple. Clear.
  3. Use Auto-Responders — Slack status: "🎯 Deep Work until 10:00." Email auto-reply kalau urgent.
  4. Start Small — Mulai dengan 1 deep work block per day. Naikin gradually to 2-3 setelah 2 minggu.
  5. The 10-Minute Rule — Kalau someone asks for your time during a block, say: "Can we do this in 10 minutes? If not, let's schedule it."

📅 Calendar Integration Tips

Google Calendar / Outlook Setup

  • Color coding: Red = Deep Work, Blue = Meetings, Yellow = Buffer, Green = Recovery
  • Recurring blocks: Set recurring events for daily deep work at your peak hours
  • Default duration: Set default meeting length to 25 min (bukan 30) atau 50 min (bukan 60)
  • "Focus Time" feature: Google Calendar has built-in Focus Time that auto-declines conflicts
  • Meeting-free days: Block 1-2 full days per week sebagai "No Meeting Day" recurring event

🔋 Energy Boosters & Drains

✅ Energy Boosters

  • 10-min walk (especially outside)
  • Cold water on face/wrists
  • Power nap 10-20 min (not longer!)
  • Music change (upbeat for energy)
  • Quick social interaction
  • Protein-rich snack
  • 5 deep breaths / box breathing
  • Change physical position/location

❌ Energy Drains

  • Back-to-back meetings (3+ hours)
  • Decision fatigue (too many small decisions)
  • Context switching every 15 min
  • Sugar crash (heavy carb lunch)
  • Doomscrolling / social media
  • Unresolved conflict/stress
  • Poor sleep (< 7 hours)
  • Working past your shutdown time

🔄 Async vs Sync Decision Framework

Kapan SYNC (meeting)?

Focus & Deep Work Protocol

Deep work is like a superpower in our economy. If you can produce elite-level work, you'll thrive. If you can't, you'll be left behind.

🏗️ Environment Setup Checklist

🏠 WFH Focus Setup

  • Dedicated workspace (bukan di kasur!)
  • Door closed atau "Do Not Disturb" sign
  • Phone di room lain / drawer
  • Noise-canceling headphones ON
  • Water bottle full di desk
  • Room temperature comfortable (22-24°C)
  • Proper lighting (natural > fluorescent)
  • Clean desk — only current task materials
  • Focus playlist ready (lo-fi, ambient, binaural)
  • All social media tabs CLOSED

🏢 Office Focus Setup

  • Book quiet room / find quiet corner
  • Headphones = universal "don't disturb" signal
  • Slack status: "🎯 Focus Mode until [time]"
  • Laptop only — close extra monitors
  • Notepad for "interrupt log" (capture later)
  • Snacks + water pre-prepared
  • Calendar blocked as "Busy"
  • Notify nearest colleagues: "Deep work next 90 min"
  • Phone face-down, silent
  • One task, one window, one goal

🎯 The 90-Minute Deep Work Protocol

Step-by-Step Protocol

  1. Pre-Session Setup (5 min) — Decide exactly what you'll work on. Write it down: "In this session, I will ___." Close everything else. Set 90-min timer.
  2. Warm-Up Phase (10 min) — Start with the easiest part of the task. Review notes, re-read last progress. Get your brain into context.
  3. Peak Focus Phase (60 min) — Full immersion. No breaks, no checking. If distraction thought comes, write it on "later" list and return immediately.
  4. Cool-Down Phase (10 min) — Start wrapping up. Leave a "breadcrumb" — write where you stopped and next step. Future-you will thank you.
  5. Completion (5 min) — Stand up. Walk. Drink water. Log progress in Notion. Celebrate the session — you just did what 95% of people can't.

🚫 How to Say No (Without Being a Jerk)

  5. "The honest no" — "I appreciate you thinking of me, but I'm at capacity this week. Let's revisit next week?"

Troubleshooting

Common Problems & Practical Solutions

🔧 Problem #1: "I Can't Stick to My Plan"

Root Causes:

  • Plan terlalu ambitious (over-scheduling)
  • No buffer time — satu delay cascading
  • External interruptions tak terduga
  • Willpower reliance (bukan system reliance)

Solutions:

  1. Plan at 70% capacity — Sisakan 30% untuk unexpected. Kalau nggak ada interrupt, bonus free time.
  2. Add buffer blocks — 15 min antara setiap major block. Non-negotiable.
  3. Use "If-Then" rules — "If meeting overruns → I still protect my next deep work block by cutting shallow work."
  4. Track deviations — Note WHY you deviated. Patterns will emerge. Fix the system, not yourself.
  5. Shrink the plan — Dari MIT 3, turunin ke MIT 1 dulu. Consistency > ambition.

🔧 Problem #2: "Too Many Meetings"

Root Causes:

  • Default culture = meeting for everything
  • FOMO — takut miss information
  • No clear async alternatives established
  • Calendar Tetris — orang book slot kosong lo

Solutions:

  1. Block focus time FIRST — Before others can book. Recurring blocks = claimed territory.
  2. Propose async alternative — "Can we do this via Slack thread? I'll respond by [time]."
  3. Decline with value — "I'm not needed here, but happy to review notes and add async input."
  4. Shorten all meetings — 25 min instead of 30. 50 min instead of 60. Set default in calendar settings.
  5. Batch meetings — All meetings on Tue/Thu. Mon/Wed/Fri = meeting-free (or mostly).

🔧 Problem #3: "WFH Distractions"

Root Causes:

  • No dedicated workspace (kerja di living room/bed)
  • Family/roommates interrupting
  • Phone/social media dalam reach
  • No clear work "mode" boundary

Solutions:

  1. Create physical boundary — Satu spot khusus kerja. Even kalau cuma 1 meja kecil. Brain associates space with mode.
  2. Communicate schedule — Share jadwal ke family/roommates. "09-12 = jangan ganggu kecuali darurat."
  3. Phone quarantine — Phone di drawer/room lain selama deep work. Out of sight = out of mind.
  4. Use app blockers — Freedom, Cold Turkey, atau Screen Time limits. Remove choice from the equation.
  5. "Work mode" ritual — Put on shoes. Play specific playlist. Open Notion. Ritual = brain trigger for focus.

🔧 Problem #4: "Burnout Signals"

Warning Signs:

  • Consistently low energy di Daily Log (3+ days in a row)
  • Dreading tasks that used to excite you
  • Working past shutdown time regularly
  • Mood = Frustrated/Tired for the whole week
  • Can't focus even during "peak" hours
  • Sunday anxiety about Monday

Recovery Protocol:

  1. Immediate: Cut workload by 50% — Only MIT 1 for 1 week. Everything else can wait or be delegated.
  2. Sleep audit — Are you getting 7-8 hours? Fix this FIRST. Nothing works without sleep.
  3. No-work evening rule — Zero work after shutdown time. No email "quick check."
  4. Add recovery blocks — 30-60 min walks, nature, exercise. Non-negotiable.
  5. Take a real day off — Not sick day where you still check email. REAL off. Phone off. Nature. Rest.
  6. If persists > 2 weeks — Talk to manager / seek professional support. Burnout is clinical — it's not weakness.

🔧 Problem #5: "Switching Between Office/Home is Chaotic"

Root Causes:

  • Different setup at each location (tools, monitors, etc.)
  • No transition ritual between modes
  • Information scattered (some at office, some at home)
  • Different routines causing cognitive load

Solutions:

  1. Cloud-first everything — Notion (not local files), Google Drive (not desktop), browser bookmarks (not local). Access anywhere.
  2. Identical core setup — Same headphones, same charger, same notebook at both locations. Reduce friction to zero.
  3. "Commute transition" ritual — Leaving office: 5-min journal on phone (what's pending). Arriving home: 10-min decompress before anything.
  4. "Travel bag" always packed — Charger, headphones, notebook, snacks — always in bag. Never scramble morning-of.
  5. Day-type templates — Different schedule for office vs WFH already planned. No decision-making needed in the morning.

FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

10 Pertanyaan yang Paling Sering Ditanya

Q1: "Gue bukan orang yang organized. Bisa nggak pakai sistem ini?"

A: Absolutely. Sistem ini designed untuk orang yang BELUM organized. Lo nggak perlu jadi "productivity junkie" dulu. Start dengan Daily Log + 1 MIT per day. Itu aja. Organisasi itu skill yang di-build, bukan personality trait. Give it 2 weeks — lo bakal surprised.

Q2: "Harus pakai Notion? Kalau pakai tools lain gimana?"

A: Notion itu recommended because it's the most flexible. Tapi SEMUA konsep di ebook ini applicable ke tools lain: Obsidian, Todoist, Asana, bahkan Google Sheets + Calendar. Yang penting adalah SISTEM-nya, bukan tools-nya. Adapt the framework to your preferred tool.

Q3: "Berapa lama sampai sistem ini jadi autopilot?"

A: Roughly: Week 1-2 = Conscious effort (feels clunky). Week 3-4 = Getting smoother. Week 5-8 = Mostly automatic. After 2 months = Feels weird WITHOUT the system. Consistency > perfection. Nggak perlu perfect execution — just show up daily.

Q4: "Boss gue yang schedule meetings. Gue nggak bisa control calendar."

A: Lo lebih punya control dari yang lo pikir. (1) Block focus time BEFORE boss can book it. (2) Communicate: "I'm most productive in mornings — can we do afternoon meetings?" (3) Propose async alternatives. Most bosses respect boundaries kalau lo frame it as "I deliver better work this way."

Q5: "Ini terlalu banyak. Overwhelmed. Mulai dari mana?"

A: ONLY do this: (1) Create Daily Log database. (2) Set 1 MIT every morning. (3) Do 1 Deep Work block (even just 45 min). That's it. Literally three things. Do those for 2 weeks. Then add more. The 30-Day Roadmap di Bab 12 breaks it down step by step.

Q6: "Gimana kalau hari gue nggak sesuai template? (e.g., urgent meeting mendadak)"

A: Templates itu guidelines, bukan rules keras. The real question is: setelah interrupt, bisa nggak lo RETURN to the plan? That's the skill. Urgent meeting muncul → attend → lalu BACK to your MIT. Don't let one disruption derail the whole day. Use buffer blocks for overflow.

Q7: "Gue kerja di timezone berbeda sama team. Hybrid + timezone = double chaos."

A: Async-first approach jadi even more penting. (1) Write down everything — no verbal-only decisions. (2) Use Loom for updates yang need context. (3) Have 1-2 overlap hours dedicated to sync communication. (4) Rest of day = your own optimal schedule based on YOUR timezone dan energy. Ini sebenarnya advantage — less meetings!

Q8: "Anak/keluarga di rumah. WFH = work from chaos."

A: Real talk — ini hard mode. Solutions: (1) Negotiate "focus hours" with partner/family (e.g., 09-11 = don't disturb unless emergency). (2) Use early morning before family wakes up for deep work. (3) Accept that your WFH day won't look like a single person's WFH day — and that's OK. Optimize around constraints, don't fight them.

Q9: "Kapan tau sistem ini perlu di-overhaul?"

A: Signs it's time to redesign: (1) Weekly review score consistently < 3 for 3+ weeks. (2) You're avoiding opening Notion/your system. (3) Role/job changed significantly. (4) You're maintaining the system MORE than using it. Rule: quarterly light review, annual full overhaul. Systems should evolve with you.

Q10: "Ini free? Atau ada upsell? What's the catch?"

A: No catch. Ebook ini comprehensive dan self-contained. Lo nggak perlu beli apa-apa lagi. If you want the pre-built Notion template (one-click duplicate), follow @yanzy17 for updates. But honestly, building it yourself from the specs in Bab 2 teaches you MORE and makes it truly yours. Build > Buy.

1️⃣3️⃣5️⃣ The 1-3-5 Rule

Daily task limit for realistic planning

  • 1 Big Thing — Your MIT, the non-negotiable
  • 3 Medium Things — Important but not giant
  • 5 Small Things — Quick wins, admin, replies

Total: 9 items max/day. If you finish all = great day. Even finishing the 1 big thing = successful day.

🎯 MIT (Most Important Tasks)

Focus on impact over activity

  • Choose 1-3 MITs each morning
  • Do MIT 1 FIRST (before email, before Slack)
  • If only MIT 1 gets done, the day is still a win
  • MITs should move projects forward, not just maintain

⏰ Time Boxing vs Time Blocking

Two related but different concepts

  • Time Blocking: Reserve time for a CATEGORY of work (e.g., "Deep Work 09-11")
  • Time Boxing: Assign a fixed time LIMIT to a specific task (e.g., "Write report — max 45 min")
  • Combine both: Block the category, then time-box individual tasks within it

🔄 GTD (Getting Things Done) Simplified

David Allen's framework in 5 steps

  1. Capture — Get everything out of your head into a system
  2. Clarify — Is it actionable? What's the next action?
  3. Organize — Put it in the right list/project/calendar
  4. Reflect — Weekly review to keep system current
  5. Engage — Do the work with confidence in your system

🧠 Eat the Frog

Mark Twain's productivity principle

"If it's your job to eat a frog, it's best to do it first thing in the morning."

  • Your "frog" = the task you're most likely to procrastinate on
  • Do it FIRST. Before anything else.
  • After the frog, everything else feels easy
  • Combines perfectly with MIT — your MIT 1 IS your frog

Notion Formula Cheatsheet

Copy-Paste Formulas untuk Hybrid Planner System

📊 Weekly Focus Hours Calculator

Rollup formula untuk sum focus hours per minggu

prop("Daily Logs").map(current.prop("Focus Hours")).sum()

Set as Rollup property di Weekly Review database, linked to Daily Log relation.

📅 Days Until Deadline

Auto-calculate days remaining di Project Tracker

dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days")

Shows negative number kalau overdue — great for urgency awareness.

🔥 Habit Streak Status

Visual indicator based on streak length

if(prop("Current Streak") >= 21, "🔥 Legendary", if(prop("Current Streak") >= 7, "⚡ On Fire", if(prop("Current Streak") >= 3, "✨ Building", "🌱 Starting")))

📈 Progress Percentage (Auto-calculated)

Based on completed sub-tasks

round(prop("Tasks Done") / prop("Total Tasks") * 100)

🎯 Daily Score Calculation

Composite score dari multiple factors

round((if(prop("MIT 1 Done"), 40, 0) + if(prop("MIT 2 Done"), 30, 0) + if(prop("MIT 3 Done"), 20, 0) + if(prop("Shutdown Done"), 10, 0)) / 10, 1)

Score out of 10. MIT 1 = 4 points, MIT 2 = 3, MIT 3 = 2, Shutdown routine = 1.

📊 Week Day Auto-Label

Auto-show day of week from date

formatDate(prop("Date"), "dddd") + " — " + formatDate(prop("Date"), "D MMM YYYY")

Output example: "Monday — 15 Jun 2026"

⏰ Meeting Load Warning

Alert when too many meetings in a day

if(prop("Meeting Count") > 5, "🔴 OVERLOAD — Cancel something!", if(prop("Meeting Count") > 3, "🟡 Heavy — Protect remaining time", "🟢 Manageable"))

Quick-Start Checklists

Print-friendly checklists untuk reference cepat

🌅 Morning Routine Checklist

  • Wake up (no snooze)
  • No phone for first 15 min
  • Water (500ml minimum)
  • Movement (stretch/walk/exercise) 10+ min
  • Shower & get dressed (even WFH!)
  • Open Notion → Daily Log
  • Set today's MIT 1-2-3
  • Check calendar (meetings today)
  • Set Day Type badge
  • Rate current energy level
  • Start Deep Work Block #1

🌙 Shutdown Routine Checklist

  • Stop ALL work tasks
  • Update Daily Log (completion, mood, win)
  • Clear inbox to zero (or note pending items)
  • Set tomorrow's MIT 1 (pre-decide)
  • Close all work tabs/apps
  • Update Slack status to "Offline"
  • Put phone in "evening mode"
  • Physical transition (change clothes, walk)
  • Say out loud: "Workday is DONE."
  • Do something non-work (cook, exercise, family)

📅 Sunday Planning Checklist

  • Open Weekly Review template
  • Score last week (1-5)
  • Write 3 wins from last week
  • Write 1 biggest lesson
  • Brain dump (5 min timer)
  • Scan next week's calendar
  • Assign Day Types (Office/WFH/Flex)
  • Set 3 Weekly Outcomes
  • Block Deep Work in calendar
  • Prep Monday's MIT 1
  • Pack bag if office day tomorrow
  • Relax — planning done! 🎉

🏠 WFH Setup Checklist

  • Dedicated workspace ready
  • Chair ergonomics okay
  • Monitor at eye level
  • Good lighting (natural preferred)
  • Headphones charged
  • Water bottle filled
  • Snacks pre-prepared
  • Phone out of reach
  • Door closed / sign up
  • Focus playlist ready
  • App blocker activated
  • Notion open, Daily Log ready

🖨️ Pro Tip: Print These

Print halaman ini dan tempel di dekat workspace lo. Physical reminders work better than digital ones ketika lo baru building habits. After 30 days, lo nggak butuh checklist lagi — it's automatic.
