# WHY HOOKS MATTER — The Brutal Math

The first line of your content does **80% of the work**. If it fails, the next 999 words die unread.

Average attention spans on social platforms in 2026:

| Platform | Average attention until scroll |
|---|---|
| TikTok / Reels | 1.7 seconds |
| Twitter/X | 2.3 seconds |
| Instagram feed | 2.6 seconds |
| LinkedIn | 3.1 seconds |
| YouTube thumbnail | 1.4 seconds |

You have **less than 3 seconds** to earn the next 30. That's the entire job of a hook.

The good news: viral content isn't random. It follows **patterns**. This book contains 100 of those patterns, ready to deploy.

# BONUS — THE 7 MASTER HOOK FORMULAS

Every hook in this book maps to one of these 7 patterns. Master them, and you can write hooks forever.

### 1. The Curiosity Gap
> *Open a loop the brain must close.*
- Template: "The reason [X] never [Y] isn't what you think."
- Why it works: triggers compulsive completion bias.

### 2. The Specific Number
> *Specific numbers feel real. Round numbers feel made up.*
- Template: "I tried [number] [things]. Only [smaller number] worked."
- Why it works: specificity = credibility.

### 3. The Contrarian Claim
> *Bold opposite of common wisdom = pattern interrupt.*
- Template: "Everyone says [popular advice]. They're wrong. Here's why:"
- Why it works: the brain pauses to resolve conflict.

### 4. The Personal Confession
> *Vulnerability + story = trust.*
- Template: "I [embarrassing or expensive thing]. Here's what I learned:"
- Why it works: humans bond over honest mistakes.

### 5. The Specific Outcome
> *Show the destination so they want to learn the journey.*
- Template: "From [low state] to [high state] in [time]. Here's how:"
- Why it works: aspiration triggers attention.

### 6. The Question Hook
> *A sharp question forces the brain to engage.*
- Template: "What if [common belief] is actually [opposite]?"
- Why it works: questions are open loops the brain wants to close.

### 7. The Save-This Promise
> *Position your content as a future-self resource.*
- Template: "Save this. You'll need it later."
- Why it works: utility framing increases bookmarks + replays.

# BONUS — 7 COMMON MISTAKES THAT KILL HOOKS

1. **Starting with greeting fluff.** "Hey guys, what's up!" → instant scroll. Cut it.
2. **Burying the lead.** If your insight is in line 5, line 1 better be a hook for line 5.
3. **Vague generalities.** "Some thoughts on success" → forgettable. "Why I quit at month 8" → unforgettable.
4. **Claiming everything is "the truth"**. If everything is the truth nothing is. Save it for your strongest takes.
5. **Starting every hook with "I".** Audience-first beats author-first. "You're doing X wrong" > "I do X this way."
6. **Using AI-generated buzzwords.** "Game-changer", "unlock", "level up" — AI smell. Use plain English.
7. **Posting and forgetting.** Hook A/B testing is everything. Same content, 5 different hooks, see which earns the next 30 seconds.
